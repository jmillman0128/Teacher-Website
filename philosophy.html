Teaching Philosophy

The beliefs & values that guide the Star Mill

Every Student Is a Star

At the heart of everything in the Star Mill is a simple belief: every student has unique potential waiting to be discovered. My role as an educator is not to fill empty vessels, but to ignite the light that already exists in each learner.

"We are made of star-stuff. We are a way for the universe to know itself." — Carl Sagan

Lifelong Learning

My teaching philosophy is heavily inspired by my time in Finland and centered on the idea of lifelong learning. As a teacher, my priority is to show students that no skill is more powerful than the ability to learn. Learning does not begin and end in the classroom... it can happen anywhere, so long as we remain open to it.

"Lifelong learning is rooted in the integration of learning and living, covering learning activities for people of all ages (children, young people, adults and the elderly, girls and boys, women and men), in all life-wide contexts (family, school, the community, the workplace, and so on) and through a variety of modalities (formal, non-formal and informal), which, together, meet a wide range of learning needs and demands." — UNESCO Institute for Lifelong Learning

Technology in Education

Technology is a major pillar of my teaching philosophy. My experiences as both a researcher and international graduate student helped me develop a wide range of technical skills, including coding, data analysis, artificial intelligence, and human-computer interaction. Because of this, I view technology not only as a classroom tool, but also as an essential outcome of modern education.

When used thoughtfully, technology can create learning experiences that traditional classrooms alone cannot achieve. At the same time, digital and information literacy skills are increasingly important in both higher education and today's workforce. By integrating technology into my lessons, I aim to help students develop the creativity, adaptability, and 21st-century competencies needed to thrive in a rapidly changing world.
