I am a New Jersey native who studied astrophysics at Franklin & Marshall College. After graduating, I completed fellowships at Fermilab and Brookhaven National Laboratory, contributing to cutting-edge research in areas such as quantum computing and stellar simulations.

Over time, I realized that while research was exciting, it wasn't the impact I wanted to have. Education had always been important to me, and I found myself drawn to it through tutoring students from K–12 through college in physics, computer science, engineering, and other STEM fields. Those experiences made it clear that helping students learn and grow was where I could make the greatest difference.

To deepen my understanding of education, I trained at the University of Jyväskylä—Finland's original teacher training institution—through the EDUMA Master's Programme. There, I focused on 21st-century challenges and innovation in education, conducting international research on the use of artificial intelligence, digital learning, and virtual reality in the classroom.
